Pan-Pontian Youth Awareness 2014

It is with great pleasure that we invite you to Chicago for the 4th Youth Awareness Convention to be hosted by the Pan Pontian Federation of the USA-Canada on Labor Day weekend from Friday, August 29 through Sunday August 31, 2014.

 

Please pre-register here:


 

Youth Awareness Weekend


 

Program information listed below*


PROGRAM

 

Friday, August 29, 2014

Registration at the Courtyard Chicago Hotel - 3-6 pm

Registration and Welcoming Glendi - 7pm-2 am Cultural Center of Hellenic American Community (Closed event - open only to participants who have registered for the convention)

 

Saturday, August 30, 2014

8:30am-3:30pm – Genocide Teaching Guide Seminar for Educators at Courtyard Hotel.


8:30-8:50 a.m. Ron Levitsky - Welcome/Introduction/Purposes of workshop
8:50-9:35 a.m. Anastasia Skoupas - “The Forgotten Genocide”
9:35-10:00 a.m. George Mavropoulos and Ron Levitsky - Introduce AMPHRC, resources, & teaching unit.
10:00-10:10 a.m. Break
10:10-10:40 a.m. Kelley Szany - “Context & Complicity: Teaching Genocide in the 21st Century Classroom”
10:40-11:30 a.m. Ron Levitsky - “Personal Testimonies” – Dr. Bartrop DVD clip (“Considering Genocide Testimony”).
Begin small group activity using personal testimonies
to create a lesson.
11:30-12:30 p.m. Lunch / review display of resources/connect
12:30-1:15 p.m. Peter Chresanthakes - “Anatolia College: 1886-1921”
1:15-2:00 p.m. Anastasia Skoupas - “Ottoman Genocide in Literature” – Tamama, Not Even My Name
2:00-3:00 p.m. Ron Levitsky - Continue small group work to create lesson.
3:00-3:15 p.m. Briefly share lessons.
3:15-3:30 p.m. Anastasia Skoupas and Ron Levitsky - Questions / Closure / Evaluation

 

10am-3pm – Youth Workshops on Pontian culture and
history at Meridian Banquet Hall.
(shuttle will leave hotel at 9:30 a.m. and return at 3:30 p.m.)

10:00 - 10:30 a.m. Introductions / Ice breaker session
10:30 - 11:00 a.m. "Anatolia College 1886-1921" Peter Chresanthakes, CFA Vice President of Institutional Advancement, Anatolia College

11:00 - 11:30 a.m. PowerPoint Presentation on the two Teaching Units about Hellenism in Asia Minor and the Ottoman Greek Genocide (presented by members of the Pontian Greek Society's youth group)
11:30 - 12:00 p.m. (Q and A and other activity based on Teaching Unit)
12:00 - 12:30 p.m. "Muhabeti or Parakathi: Pontic memory and identity as singing dialogues"
Yannis Tsekouras, PhD candidate from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign
12:30-1:30 p.m. Lunch
1:30 - 3:00 p.m. Dance workshops

 

12:30-4pm - Pan-Pontian Federation Meetings at Courtyard Hotel

6pm-1am - Dinner Dance at the Meridian featuring Kostas Fetfatsidis (vocals and lyra) and Peter Skoupas (ntaouli) (shuttle will be available to and from hotel)

 

Sunday, August 31, 2014

9am -12pm - Church Service at St. John Greek Orthodox Church

2-5pm - Dance and theatrical performance at St. John Greek Orthodox Church
